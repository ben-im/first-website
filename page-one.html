Anime for Noobs

History

Anime is almost always based on a previously written manga. Manga is a japanese form of comic or drawn story. Modern anime first began in 1956 and has made many advancements with animation studios utilizing many tools such as CGI, hand-drawing, and computer-assisted drawing. Although traditionally voiced over in Japanese, most anime can be viewed in a variety of languages. Anime comes in many forms such as shorts, 20-30 minute episodes, and full length movies. It is so diverse, you most likely can find one for almost any concept you can think of.

Genres

There are more genres of anime than you can think of. Whether it be a story about a young protagonist's growth as they face new enemies, a story about a protagonist's everyday life and daily activities, a story based on horror and thrill, or whatever else you can think of, it probably exists. There is something out there for everyone in the world of anime. Many people think that anime is not for them, but anime is just a form of media and any concept you can think of probably exists. To give an idea of how obscure these shows and films can get, there is a very popular anime about a boy who goes to the most prestigious school for cooking and must battle other students to claim to top spot.

Growing Popularity

Anime has been airing to western audiences for the past few decades through television programs such as Toonami. In recent years it has blown up in popularity with the growth of the internet and streaming services. As a few extremely popular anime have exploded in popularity, such as Attack on Titan and Naruto, this has led people to discover many more unique shows and helped people broaden their horizons. Anime movies are even being shown in theatres all over the world now and the authors and animation companies are having great success.
